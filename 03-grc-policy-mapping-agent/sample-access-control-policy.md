# Sample Access Control Policy

## Document Purpose

This fictional policy defines baseline access control expectations for identity governance, role-based access, privileged access, access reviews, and audit evidence.

This document is for portfolio demonstration only and does not represent a real company policy.

## Policy Scope

This policy applies to fictional employees, contractors, service accounts, business applications, administrative tools, and systems that store or process business data.

## Policy Statements

### AC-01: Access Must Be Role-Based

User access must be assigned based on approved job role, department, and business need. Access must follow the principle of least privilege.

### AC-02: Access Requests Must Be Approved

New access, modified access, and elevated access requests must be approved by the appropriate manager or system owner before provisioning.

### AC-03: Joiner-Mover-Leaver Events Must Be Reviewed

Access must be reviewed during onboarding, job changes, department transfers, and termination events. Access that is no longer required must be removed timely.

### AC-04: Terminated Users Must Be Deprovisioned

Access for terminated users must be disabled or removed according to the organization's offboarding requirements. Deprovisioning evidence must be retained.

### AC-05: Privileged Access Must Be Restricted

Administrative and privileged access must be limited to authorized users with a documented business need. Privileged access must be reviewed more frequently than standard access.

### AC-06: Segregation of Duties Must Be Enforced

Users must not have conflicting permissions that allow them to initiate and approve the same high-risk business process without independent review.

### AC-07: Access Reviews Must Be Performed Periodically

User access must be reviewed on a periodic basis to confirm that access remains appropriate. Review results, exceptions, and remediation actions must be documented.

### AC-08: Access Exceptions Must Be Documented

Temporary or exception-based access must include business justification, approval, expiration date, and risk acceptance when required.

### AC-09: Audit Evidence Must Be Retained

Evidence of access approvals, access reviews, deprovisioning, exception approvals, and remediation must be retained for audit and compliance review.

### AC-10: AI-Assisted Access Review Must Require Human Approval

AI agents may assist with access review analysis, policy mapping, exception identification, and evidence organization. AI agents must not approve access, remove access, grant access, or accept risk without human approval.

## Policy Owner

Fictional Information Security and GRC Team

## Review Frequency

This policy should be reviewed annually or when major IAM, GRC, audit, or AI governance requirements change.
